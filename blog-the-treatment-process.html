Group De-Addiction Counselling Therapy

The group therapy process of counselling involves the process of providing the general awareness of all the types of addictions to the patients as a comprehensive treatment program to make them understand the symptoms and severity of the disease they are struggling with. The addictions include Alcohol, Marijuana (Ganja), Hash (Charas), Cocaine, Heroin (Smack or Brown Sugar), M.D., LSD, and other psychodalic substances. Along with that, other addictions like Gambling, Nicotine, Mobile and Internet Addiction and many other.

This therapy includes the following topics in the program:

  • General Awareness regarding the drug or the concerned addiction.
  • Scientific audio-visual explanation of the working of the brain function for a complete mental & psychological upliftment to make the patients believe about the real biological fact behind the addiction.
  • Sharing sessions with other fellow patients, the story of their addiction career from the time they started their addiction as an enjoyment material till their struggle with the addiction, unmanageability of their lives with other fellow patients.
  • Explanation and training sessions on how to quit the drug abuse, working on the solution of quitting the addiction along with the problems and consequences that are associated with it in life.
  • The traits and characters of the person who is an addict and how to change their own physical and mental obsession for the addiction.

The Group Therapy Counseling in this addiction rehabilitation really and objectively works in the process as it makes the patients feel free to share their innermost problems, insecurities, character without any hesitation to the crowd in the group where each one of them has gone through the same physical and mental illness in their lives. This makes the addicted person freed from his mind through sharing process.

One to One Patient Counseling Therapy

This therapy includes the following topics in the program:

The one-to-one counseling therapy process of providing personal counselling sessions with well qualified psychologists and counsellors who were themselves an ex-addict personalities of their own times.

Their past experiences with the addiction, then how they quitted their drug or alcohol addiction and make the currently addict patient believe the miracles of the life one experiences after quitting.

The power of the willingness to quit, through psychological treatment and spiritual belief.
